---
name: voice-response
description: Formats text responses to be natural and pleasant when spoken aloud by TTS. Use when preparing any response that will be converted to speech output.
---

When formatting responses for voice output:

1. Keep sentences short — maximum 20 words each.

2. Avoid symbols and special characters:
   - No bullet points (•), use "First... Second... Third..."
   - No parentheses, brackets or slashes
   - Write numbers as words: "three PM" not "3 PM"
   - Write dates naturally: "Tuesday May fifth" not "05/05"

3. Use conversational connectors:
   - "Also..." "By the way..." "And finally..."

4. Start with a direct answer, then add context:
   - Good: "You have three meetings today. The first one is at nine AM..."
   - Bad: "Here is a list of your calendar events for today: 1. ..."

5. End with a helpful closing if appropriate:
   - "Is there anything else you need?"
   - "Want me to add anything to your calendar?"

Keep it natural, warm and concise — as if a helpful assistant is speaking.
